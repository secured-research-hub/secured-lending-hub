FGTS
Recurrence & One Click · Market Saturation — Q2 2025 to Q1 2026
Most recent study: Q1 2026 · N=1,134

What is the product

FGTS Saque-Aniversário allows CLT workers to anticipate their severance fund balance, receiving cash immediately in exchange for an interest rate. Competes directly with credit cards and personal loans and is perceived as "the user's own money."

Market dynamics

  • Caixa dominates first hire; Nubank leads in recurrence
  • Inter and Meu Tudo growing fast among recurring Mass Market users
  • ~40% of Nubank clients switch banks on the second hire

Audience profile

  • Mass Market (57% recurring) · 30–49 years · indebted and credit-restricted
  • 52% female · Southeast (50%), Northeast (19%)
  • Bank app is the main hiring channel (59% Mass Market)
  • High post-hiring satisfaction (83%) but loyalty is conditional on price

Market Saturation Q1/2026 · SEM Model

Hiring intent is not solely driven by price — product trust (63%) and emotional security (30%) carry significant weight. Rate is an important hygiene factor, but not the only motivator. For recurring users who compare frequently, rate and available credit become more decisive over time.

Market Saturation Q1/2026 · SEM Model

Recurring users are willing to accept higher fees in exchange for convenience — if the amount is urgent or they're already anticipating frequently with the same bank, familiarity with the process reduces price sensitivity.

Recurrence & One Click Q2/2025 · N=12
High rate perception vs. low net amount — main hiring barrier (55%). Users see the rate in % but don't understand the real budget impact. Drives abandonment and competitor comparison, especially among non-recurring users who actively compare.

Conversion

One Click is the bare minimum recurring users expect. Repeating the full journey every time is perceived as a lack of recognition, not extra security. Non-recurring users also value it, but prefer to check available balance first before skipping steps.

Recurrence & One Click Q2/2025 · N=12

Hiring is predominantly digital — the bank app is the primary channel (56% overall; 59% Mass Market recurring). Speed to cash is the key conversion lever: making time-to-cash explicit and tangible reduces drop-off at the final step.

Market Saturation Q1/2026 · N=1,134
Full journey repeated on every hire — recurring users with active CNPJ authorization must redo all steps. This creates unnecessary friction, adds excessive steps to the funnel, and accelerates churn to competitors with simpler flows.

Management

Loyalty is sustained by belonging (27%), positive experience (22%), and well-rated product (16%). Competitive rate has only 4% weight in retention — but its absence breaks everything. The product must deliver practical recognition (discounts, better conditions) as a mandatory baseline, not a bonus.

Market Saturation Q1/2026 · SEM Model

Recurrence is driven by rate, credit amount, and ecosystem integration. Once familiarity is established, users become more economically rational. Recurring users actively compare rates and available credit. Ecosystem integration (FGTS advance as part of a broader financial offer) increases convenience and perceived value.

Private Payroll
Drop & Success Cases · Market Deep Dive — Q2 2025 to Q4 2025
Most recent study: Q4 2025 · Qualitative + Deep Dive

What is the product

Private payroll loan with direct paycheck deduction for CLT private sector workers. Objectively safer and cheaper than personal loans — but suffers from structural invisibility. The majority of eligibles don't know the product exists at Nubank or anywhere in the market.

The awareness problem

  • 88% of eligibles have never had a Private Payroll offer at Nubank
  • Discovery is accidental: app notification, TikTok, word of mouth
  • 54% are CLT but only 19% are active product users
  • Personal loan is the mental benchmark — everything is compared to it

Who converts and why

  • 88% of users are CLT driven by financial urgency — covering bills, avoiding late payments
  • Main reason to choose PP: lower interest rate (39%), followed by easier installments (28%)
  • 46% of users simulated other loans at Nubank before hiring PP
  • 70% are on their first ever private payroll loan — still a new category

Discovery is accidental, not intentional. Most users find the product through a push notification, TikTok video, or a friend's recommendation — not by actively searching. Before hiring, knowledge is fragmented and superficial: users know the discount is applied to payroll, that the payment is fixed, and that it's "cheaper than a credit card" — but that's it.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025

CTPS Digital is a growing acquisition channel — especially for smaller fintechs (Gooroo, Agibank, Meu Tudo). The app provides institutional validation and directs users via a link, converting clients who would never reach Nubank organically. For larger banks, CTPS informs about credit availability and enables comparison.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025

Eligibles have a lower-risk financial profile — organized, no immediate credit need, and a negative view of loans in general. Users are mostly CLT in urgent situations. This gap explains why awareness doesn't automatically convert into hiring.

Nuzaré Wave 3 · N=571 users / 426 eligibles
Product unknown vs. personal loan — PL is the mental benchmark for everyone. Private payroll feels "more complicated" even though it's objectively better. The battle is about familiarity and mental models, not price. Users are not rejecting the product — they're not even aware of it.

Consideration is driven by stability and trust in "official" or "serious" institutions. Users evaluate the trade-off between the solidity of traditional banks and the transparency or agility of digital banks. The installment amount — "does it fit in my salary?" — is the primary filter, not the interest rate or total cost.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025

Traditional banks win on security but lose on experience. Digital banks win on UX but lose when the offer isn't visible. Fintechs win on immediate availability but lose on trust. The payroll loan is seen as advantageous in any institution — but reasons and risks vary by category.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025

Mass Market users compare reactively and based on familiarity — driven by urgency, emotional factors, or convenience. Super Core / High Income users compare analytically: rates, flexibility, prepayment conditions, and total effective cost across multiple institutions and banks.

Drop & Success Cases Q2/2025 · N=12
Consignable margin not understood — no profile (Mass Market to High Income) understands what margin is, how it's calculated, or why it limits installments. Generates abandonment and trial-and-error in the calculator. Users frequently leave the app to search for external explanations.
Rate shown as "starting from" creates skepticism — Super Core / High Income users immediately question the "a partir de 1.89%" message. They understand it's not the final rate and choose to simulate or compare first before proceeding, while Mass Market users don't question it at all.

Conversion

Drop is exploratory, not rejection. Many users drop out because they were curious, comparing with other banks, or simulating without urgent need. This is especially common among non-PBR users. Drop doesn't mean disinterest — it's often part of the decision-making process. The user leaves to compare and may not return.

Drop & Success Cases Q2/2025 · N=12

Conversion is driven by installment affordability + trust + frictionless experience. Fintechs convert even with inconsistent journeys when they approve negative-credit borrowers with affordable installments. For Mass Market, installment amount has emotional weight: "I want to see if I can get it" is the primary motivation at the calculator step.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025

The early repayment benefit is a powerful but invisible differentiator. Users who hired PP at Nubank value the ability to prepay without needing a manager or phone call — but this benefit only appears on the final contract screen. It's frequently missed by Mass Market users and only proactively sought by Super Core/High Income.

Drop & Success Cases Q2/2025 · Usability Test

Management

Post-hiring is the most fragmented and negative phase of the journey. Users rely on app clarity to monitor: the loan status must be transparent, installment history accessible, and the contract end date visible. When this information is unclear, it contradicts the main reasons for taking out the loan — generating insecurity and confusion.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025

The biggest post-hiring detraction driver is paycheck-app sync errors. When the installment deducted from the paycheck doesn't match what the app shows, users develop disproportionate anxiety about CPF blacklisting. This threatens all trust built during the hiring process.

Nuzaré Wave 3 · Detraction verbatims
Weak post-hiring journey across the entire market — no competitor delivers consistent clarity, proactive follow-up, or accessible support after hiring. This is a clear differentiation opportunity: lack of contract history, confusion with multiple contracts, and no alerts for contract end date are universal pain points.
Users track installments outside the bank app — via work card (CTPS) and payslip. When information diverges between the app and the payslip, users feel a "black box" effect and lose confidence in the digital channel.

Portability, Refinancing & Renegotiation

Portability doesn't register on the consumer's mental radar. Almost no one uses it because they don't know it exists, don't understand what it means, and confuse it with taking out a new loan. The option isn't clearly presented in apps — the main tool for raising awareness about financial products.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025

Refinancing and renegotiation are treated as synonyms. The goal in both cases is always the same: reduce the installment, extend the deadline, or get some immediate cash. "I need to breathe now, then I'll figure out the rest." It's a frustrating moment — users don't want to reach this stage. The trigger usually comes from trying to get another loan and discovering they can't.

Market Deep Dive Q4/2025
Portability, refinancing, and renegotiation are presented without conceptual translation — terms appear as actions/choices without explanation of what they mean or their practical consequences. Users don't know the difference and feel insecure. When they don't find them quickly in the app, they conclude the feature doesn't exist and escalate to a manager.

INSS
INSS X-Ray · Top-up Research · Assistance Benchmarking — Q3 2025 to Q1 2026
Most recent study: Nuzaré W3 · N=957 (566 users + 391 eligibles)

What is the product

Payroll loan for INSS retirees and pensioners, with automatic deduction from the benefit. The most accessible credit product for an audience with limited access to the traditional financial system — but also the hardest to reach digitally.

Audience profile

  • Retirees (78%) and pensioners (25%) · 60–70 years (51%) · up to 3 min. wages (86%)
  • ~30% of 60+ don't own a smartphone; 69% own one but only 11% feel very confident
  • Pensioners are more digital: 84% own smartphone vs. 69% of retirees
  • Primary bank: traditional banks (79–87%) · Physical branch is main hiring channel (78%)

Awareness and behavior

  • 8 in 10 eligibles never had an INSS offer at Nu
  • 44% of non-users hired elsewhere P6M — wanted PL, not available at Nu
  • Urgency growing: 60% emergency (W3) vs 52% (W2)
  • 83% of users compared offers before closing with Nu; 77% had at least one other offer

INSS X-Ray / Datafolha Q3/2025 · N=337
Rates and interest not understood — users can't translate percentage into real impact on their benefit. The simulation shows numbers, not practical consequences. When the rate is not understood, users escalate to human validation (call center, manager, trusted family member) or interrupt the simulation entirely.
Structural distrust of Nubank among 71+ — Nubank doesn't carry the "weight" of a traditional bank. 20–26% give Nubank a score of 0 on safety, solidity, and transparency. The physical branch, printed contract, and in-person manager are missing — and these are the elements that convey security for this age group.

Conversion

The simulation experience combines bank app + Meu INSS app + trusted person. The Meu INSS app is the primary source of truth for margin, active contracts, and deductions — the bank becomes an intermediary. Users explicitly say they check the INSS app first, then go to the bank. The goal isn't centralizing in Nubank's app; it's being predictable about where each piece of information lives.

Assistance Benchmarking Q1/2026

In-app support is perceived as an insufficient bot for this audience. Chat is seen as automated and unable to resolve sensitive credit doubts. Users need a human voice for loan contracts. The path from self-service to human is unclear — when users can't find it, they abandon the flow or go outside the app.

Assistance Benchmarking Q1/2026

The payroll loan is presented with many numbers but little practical translation. Users need: real cost of the decision (CET in currency, not just percentage), impact on monthly benefit, operational predictability (when the money comes in, extra fees), and the ability to simulate in their own way. When help exists but doesn't solve the doubt at the critical moment, the simulation is interrupted.

Assistance Benchmarking Q1/2026

Management

Loan management is much less familiar than hiring. Because the installment is automatically deducted, most users don't actively monitor the loan — they only check when they need a new one. When they return to the app, they find no clear management, no installment history, and no end-of-contract alerts. The experience shifts to payslip + Meu INSS for any real monitoring.

Assistance Benchmarking Q1/2026

Loan management in digital is varied and inconsistent. When it works, it delivers fluidity and speed — highly appreciated. But it exposes important weaknesses for an older, risk-averse audience: difficulty understanding costs, little control over decisions, and journeys that are interrupted or migrate between channels without clear explanation.

Assistance Benchmarking Q1/2026
Post-hiring is invisible — automatic deduction means most users don't monitor the contract. The biggest friction is cognitive: the app shows numbers but doesn't translate impacts (rates, installments, margin, schedule, total cost). Help tools are either not visible within the management screen or perceived as bots that can't resolve sensitive questions.

Portability, Refinancing & Renegotiation

9 in 10 clients prefer portability with Top-up. Ideal offer: lower installment + longer term + Top-up, even if it raises the total cost. 34% would only do portability if Top-up is available. Top-up awareness grows with experience: 77% of those with 5+ contracts know it vs. far fewer among first-timers.

Top-up Research Q3/2025 · N=809

Top-up is intuitively understood as "the money that goes into your account when you make changes to your loan" (84–89% associate this correctly), but not everyone understands the mechanism behind it. The value proposition — smaller installment + extra cash — resonates strongly across all profiles.

Top-up Research Q3/2025 · N=809

Portability, refinancing, and renegotiation are presented as actions without conceptual explanation — terms appear without translation of concepts and practical consequences. Advanced operations (portability, refinancing) rarely happen via app; they usually occur outside the digital realm — through physical branches, manager delegation, or correspondent. The main factor is not complexity but confidence to sustain the operation end-to-end without explanation gaps.

Assistance Benchmarking Q1/2026
Top-up awareness is not evenly distributed — eligibles (55% unaware) and first-time users (42% unaware) miss out on the most powerful portability incentive. Communication about Top-up needs to happen earlier in the journey, before the portability decision point.

Public X-Ray · SIAPE Deep Dive · Community Benchmarking — Q4 2024 to Q1 2026
Most recent study: Q1 2026 · N=955 + Qualitative

What is the product

Payroll loan for municipal, state, federal, and military public servants. Stable, predictable income audience — but strongly anchored to traditional banks, with preference for physical and human channels, and a clear separation between "everyday money" and "serious money."

Profiles by sphere

  • Municipal (61%): lower income (66% earn up to 3 min. wages), highest appetite for payroll loans
  • State (26%): higher wage average; personal loan could be an alternative
  • Federal civil (25%): highest wage, easier access to credit, less inclined to take loans
  • Military (18%): youngest profile, most open to Nubank — but lowest appetite for loan products

Key dynamics

  • Salary bank (where salary is received) is the #1 reason for choosing a bank to hire a payroll loan
  • Lower interest rate is #2 — and a decisive dealbreaker even for small differences (e.g., 0.06%)
  • 61% of servants up to 34 years would consider hiring a payroll loan with Nubank
  • 82% of servants up to 44 have children/relatives who use Nubank

The dominant mental model: "loans are a user-driven topic; external approaches are tolerated only if occasional, clear, and contextual."

There are four distinct SIAPE profiles: (1) PBR who hires at Nu — escaping harassment, values agility and UX; (2) Non-PBR who hires at Nu — driven by autonomy and a timely offer; (3) Non-PBR who hires at competitor — strict rate comparison, math over experience; (4) PBR who uses Nu daily but hires elsewhere — daily UX vs. safety for large, high-value commitments.

SIAPE Deep Dive Q1/2026

"Mental accounting" is the invisible barrier. Users separate "everyday money" (Nubank — agility, control, organization) from "serious money" (traditional bank — stability, long-term commitment). Nubank is the "anti-chaos" tool for daily life, but not yet perceived as a structural partner for high-value, long-term debt.

SIAPE Deep Dive Q1/2026

Trust in the institution accounts for up to 24% of the hiring decision — a strategic asset that can retain PBR clients tempted to hire elsewhere. Salary bank primacy (#1 driver) and lower interest rates (#2) are the top factors. Being perceived as an "official" or "serious" institution matters significantly for consideration.

Rate as a penny dealbreaker — a PBR who uses Nubank for everything hires at BB for a 0.06% difference. "Nubank was at 1.95% and BB at 1.89% — that was enough for me to walk away." UX doesn't compensate for the perceived mathematical disadvantage. Rational profiles treat this as a pure calculation, not a relationship decision.

Conversion

Human channel is not a substitute for digital — it's a strategic complement. Managers, correspondents, and WhatsApp are activated when they save time, translate risk, or unlock app gaps. The choice of channel is pragmatic: speed, clarity, and trust drive when users go digital vs. human. This applies to both civilians and military, for different reasons.

Community Benchmarking Q1/2026

The security paradox at conversion: Nubank's smooth UX becomes a liability for high-value contracts. "Too easy" triggers fear of fraud or error. Additional steps — biometrics, confirmations, extra validations — are well received when they reinforce protection in sensitive actions. The absence of "human friction" creates anxiety about large commitments.

SIAPE Deep Dive Q1/2026 · Community Benchmarking Q1/2026

The Banco do Brasil journey is described as the most trustworthy — not despite its extra steps, but because of them. BB redirects to WhatsApp at key points, which unexpectedly increases perceived security. Caixa's app directs users to branch WhatsApp, which also increases trust through human handoff. Contract clarity (visible PDF) builds confidence.

SIAPE Deep Dive Q1/2026 · App benchmarking
84% of servants say downloading the contract is an essential factor for hiring with Nubank. 79% want the ability to call a bank manager. For public servants, contract formalization and access to human support are not nice-to-haves — they are conversion requirements for structural financial commitments.

Management

The payroll loan is not experienced as a unique digital journey. The real experience combines: bank app (simulation and hiring), SouGov/E-Consig (margin consultation and authorization), pay stub (installment confirmation), and human channels (sensitive decisions). Users don't expect centralization — they expect predictability about where each piece of information lives and why that source is trustworthy.

Community Benchmarking Q1/2026

Payroll loan management is more about seeking better rates than monitoring installments. Since payment is automatic and confirmed on the pay stub, monthly monitoring of the bank app loses relevance. Attention becomes active when the opportunity to reduce costs arises — not for routine tracking. The paycheck and SouGov take center stage; the bank app becomes secondary.

Community Benchmarking Q1/2026
Fragmented post-hiring management — monitoring happens via pay stub, SouGov, and bank app in parallel. No single source of truth. When information diverges between sources, insecurity grows. Control limitations (not being able to adjust installments or complete portability in the app) reinforce the "black box" perception.

Portability, Refinancing & Renegotiation

Portability, refinancing, and renegotiation have conceptual interest but rarely reach clear and actionable flows. Users understand their purpose but when they don't find them quickly in the app, they conclude the feature doesn't exist and escalate to a manager. The perception reinforces channel switching and weakens trust in the digital journey — over time, the app becomes informational and execution is delegated to humans.

Community Benchmarking Q1/2026

There is significant confusion between renegotiation and refinancing — used interchangeably by users. The lack of clear distinction in the app (or the use of alternative words like "renew" or "solve debts") contributes to this. When seeking either, the goal is always the same: reduce the installment, extend the deadline, or consolidate into one payment.

Community Benchmarking Q1/2026

Portability path is varied and has multiple friction points. When found in the app, it may fail due to preconditions (prior authorizations needed at other institutions) or may not list payroll-deductible in the portability simulation. In SouGov/E-Consig, users see it as authorization/margin consultation, not as where portability is executed. When unclear, users call their manager or correspondent.

Community Benchmarking Q1/2026
Portability, refinancing, and renegotiation are presented as actions without conceptual translation — terms appear without explaining what they mean or their practical consequences. This creates uncertainty in a risk-averse audience. Nubank and BB are exceptions — identified as banks that provide some explanation of portability.
